Stone Veil

Reimagining civic infrastructure as a piece of landscape architecture — a quiet interlude along Singapore's Pang Sua Park Connector Network where users can rest, reflect, and reconnect with their surroundings.

Project Type

Public Architecure

Tools & Software

Rhino 3D, D5 Render

Year

2025

Focus

Material Expression

Project Overview

Composed of geopolymer concrete and frosted glass panels, Stone Veil explores the relationship between solidity and openness. The concrete shell grounds the building in its infrastructural setting, while the glass veil filters daylight and connects users to the landscape. Together, they balance the functional and the sensory — offering a space that feels both sheltered and connected to its surroundings.

Within, laminated timber finish and subtle tiled surfaces lend warmth and resilience. Universal design features ensure accessibility and dignity for every user. Between the concrete shell and inner volume lies a breathing void — a spatial buffer that mediates light, air, and sound, crafting a calm, ventilated interior that remains visually and physically connected to nature. Ultimately, Stone Veil turns an everyday facility into a thoughtful public space — one that blends care, comfort, and environmental awareness within the rhythms of daily life.
